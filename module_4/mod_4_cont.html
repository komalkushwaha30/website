3. Operators:-

An operator, in the context of programming, is a symbol that typically represents an action or process. These symbols, adapted from mathematics and logic, allow manipulation of values or operands. Here are some common types of operators:
Assignment Operator: Represented by the “=” sign, it assigns a value to a variable.
Arithmetic Operators: These include:
Addition (+): Adds two values.
Subtraction (-): Subtracts one value from another.
Multiplication (*): Multiplies two values.
Division (/): Divides one value by another.
Integer Division (//): Returns the quotient of division (discarding the remainder).
Modulo (%): Computes the remainder after division.
Exponentiation (^): Raises a value to a power.
Boolean Operators (also known as logical operators):
And: Represents logical conjunction.
AndAlso: Short-circuit logical AND.
OrElse: Short-circuit logical OR.
Or: Represents logical inclusion.
Not: Negates a condition.
Xor: Represents logical exclusion.
Relational Operators:
Greater Than (>)
Less Than (<) Greater Than or Equal To (>=)
Less Than or Equal To (<=) Equal To (==) Not Equal To (<>)
Is: Compares references.
Bitwise Operators: Used for manipulating binary values (not always common in programming):
Bitwise Negation (Not)
Bitwise Exclusive OR (Xor)
Bitwise AND (And)
Bitwise OR (Or)
